## Article 1: How to Prepare a Clear First Brief Before Speaking With a Lawyer

Audience: Clients  
Category: Client Guide  
Suggested byline: LearnedCircle Editorial

### Summary

Many clients know they need a lawyer, but they do not know how to explain their matter clearly. A good first brief saves time, reduces confusion and helps a lawyer understand whether they can assist.

### Draft

Before contacting a lawyer, write down the basic facts of your matter in a simple order. Start with what happened, when it happened, who was involved, what documents you have, and what outcome you want.

A good first brief does not need to be long. It should help the lawyer quickly understand the nature of the problem. For example, if the issue is a business contract, mention the parties, the date of the agreement, the disputed clause, whether payment has been made, and what remedy you are seeking.

Clients should also gather important documents before speaking with a lawyer. These may include contracts, receipts, court papers, letters, emails, identity documents, company records, tax notices or property documents.

Avoid hiding facts that may be uncomfortable. Lawyers need the full picture to advise properly. A fact that seems small to a client may be very important legally.

On LearnedCircle, clients can submit a case brief and search for lawyers by practice area, location, availability and pro bono openness. A clear brief helps the platform and the lawyer understand the matter faster.

## Article 2: How to Choose the Right Lawyer for Your Matter

Audience: Clients  
Category: Client Guide  
Suggested byline: LearnedCircle Editorial

### Summary

Choosing a lawyer should not be guesswork. Clients should look at practice area, location, experience, communication style, verification status and availability.

### Draft

The right lawyer for one matter may not be the right lawyer for another. A lawyer who handles family law may not be the best fit for a tax dispute. A corporate lawyer may not be the right fit for a criminal defence issue.

Start by identifying the area of law involved. Common areas include corporate law, tax law, property law, family law, employment law, data protection, intellectual property, litigation, arbitration and company secretarial matters.

Next, look for lawyers whose profiles show relevant experience. A verified profile, clear practice area, year of call, location, professional summary and public contact preference can help you compare options.

Clients should also consider communication. A lawyer who responds clearly and explains next steps can make the process easier. Before paying fees, ask about the scope of work, likely timeline, documents required and billing method.

LearnedCircle is built to make this process easier by helping clients find verified lawyers and contact suitable professionals directly.

## Article 3: Why Verified Lawyer Profiles Matter

Audience: Lawyers and Clients  
Category: Trust and Verification  
Suggested byline: LearnedCircle Editorial

### Summary

Verification improves trust. It helps clients identify credible profiles and helps lawyers distinguish themselves professionally.

### Draft

In a digital legal marketplace, trust is essential. Clients need confidence that they are viewing real legal professionals. Lawyers need a platform where professional credibility is protected.

Verified profiles help reduce confusion and impersonation. They can include details such as the lawyer's full name, year of call, Supreme Court Number, location, practice area, profile photo, firm name and public contact preference.

Verification does not mean a platform guarantees the outcome of a legal matter. It means the platform has taken steps to review the profile and make the lawyer's professional presence clearer.

For lawyers, verification creates visibility. It allows clients, employers, law firms and other lawyers to understand who they are and what they do. For clients, verification supports safer discovery and better comparison.

## Article 4: What Clients Should Know Before Messaging a Lawyer Online

Audience: Clients  
Category: Client Guide  
Suggested byline: LearnedCircle Editorial

### Summary

Direct messaging is useful, but clients should avoid sending unnecessary sensitive information before a lawyer-client relationship is properly established.

### Draft

Online messaging makes it easier to contact a lawyer, but clients should use it carefully. The first message should explain the matter generally without exposing unnecessary private information.

Start with the type of issue, your location, the key dates, the documents available and the help you need. For example: "I need help reviewing a tenancy agreement in Lagos" is better than sending all personal documents immediately.

Ask the lawyer whether they handle that type of matter. You may also ask about consultation fees, availability, timeline and next steps.

Clients should avoid sending passwords, bank details, confidential business secrets or sensitive documents unless the lawyer has confirmed how the information will be handled.

## Article 7: What Employers Should Include in a Legal Job Posting

Audience: Employers and Law Firms  
Category: Jobs and Opportunities  
Suggested byline: LearnedCircle Editorial

### Summary

A clear job post attracts better applicants. Employers should state role, location, practice area, experience level, work mode and application requirements.

### Draft

Legal job postings should be clear and specific. A vague opportunity attracts unsuitable applications and wastes time for both employer and applicant.

A good job post should include the role title, employer type, practice area, location, work mode, expected experience, key responsibilities, application deadline and required documents.

If the role is remote, hybrid or full time, say so. If the role requires litigation experience, corporate drafting, tax knowledge, data protection, company secretarial work or contract review, state it clearly.

Applicants should be able to submit directly and upload their CV. LearnedCircle supports job and opportunity listings to help employers reach lawyers and legal professionals more efficiently.
